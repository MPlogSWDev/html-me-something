Some influences (mostly video games...) :

As a kid i fell in love with computers...
Frist, was with Oregon Tral!

I once got in trouble for staying after school to play Oregon Trail on an Apple lle in the computer lab. My parents didn't know I was staying after class. I was in first grade and rode my bike to school every day.... Alone... Times have changed!

We got our first mac! It was black and white....

I wan't allowed to have a video game system at home so this was groundbreaking for me. This is the first Flight Simulator I remember playing. It was called "Apache Strike" and was barley playable. No instructions... Just trial and error. This is the actual gameplay graphics.

At age 10 we got the internet and our first
(non-mac) home PC!

This is Commander Keen. It was one of my earliest games I remember playing in color on our PC. Pionts are scored for jumping on sluggs with a pogo stick, collecting candy and shooting alliens with a ray gun. Enough said...

Not long after that the junk mail began...

Our first OSP was Compuserve. We had dial-up until the point I left home. Despite the limitaions there was still a ton possible... Like downloading new games and screensavers!

Wolfenstien & Doom


These are the first games I remember enjoying that are 3D first person shooter's. They where both something I downloded, I think each of them took all day... Doom was a favorite but we were still on dial-up internet. On-line gaming was completly out of the question. I spent days working on tweeking the video card and driver setings just to make them playable.

Cable Internet and Quake!

I lived for two years before turning 18 with my grandmother. She had a new Gateway and Broadband. I spent many nights experiencing
on-line gaming for the first time. Windows 98 was the current OS and .mp3's were available on Napster. At the age of 16 I couldn't have been more up to date in the computer world. I kept up on new hardware and software releases. I drempt of builing my own computer. That was all about to change...

I walked away from computers....

I moved to the west coast and became involved with my carrer in construction. Any free time was spent out in nature and with friends. I had never owned my own computer so it didn't feel like I was missing out on much. A new technology was about to make my complacency solidified.

The Smart Phone Revolution

When I left for Oregon I had a cell phone. When I moved back to the midwest in 2004 internet was available on cells. However, it wasn't untill the BlackBerry Pearl came out in 2006 that I bought my first smart phone with internet access. Over the next 14 years I upgraded many times. The access to the internet and abillity to use apps was all I needed. I always wanted to own a PC but never had the resources matched with a justifialbe use for one.

LaunchCode Revived My Intrest

After watching a friend go through this program I became aware that a new carrer path was possible for me. I never trully lost intrest in computers, I simply didn't have a purpose for them. When the pandemic hit I realised it may be a good time to consider this program as realistic. So in June of 2020 I bought my very first personal computer with the intentions of applying to LaunchCode as soon as possible. Almost a year later I'm enrolled and have just made my first HTML page... Huzzah!
